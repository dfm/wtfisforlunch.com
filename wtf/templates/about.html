Hi. I'm Dan. I fucking built this website.

It tells you where to go for lunch by mixing your current location and your past choices up in a pot and adding some magic. The data comes from the Foursquare API (which is awesome) and not from an evil company who's name rhymes with "kelp".

This service doesn't make recommendations. Instead, it makes decisions. This is great because making decisions sucks.

Did I mention that it's open source. Rock it.

How does it work?

tl;dr Magic.

I have come up with (translation: pulled out of my ass) a parametric model for the likelihood that you'll accept a suggestion that I present to you. Assuming some simple priors, I can invert this likelihood function (using Bayes) and draw "representative" restaurants from a distribution over distance, rating and price from the Foursquare API. In practice, this sampling isn't actually very rigorous. In fact, it's really kludgey as fuck but we do what we can :-).

This parametric model of the likelihood function has a few parameters (duh!) and these can obviously be different for different users. Every time you say yes or no, or rate a restaurant that you went to, this says something about your preferences. In practice, each response that you make should take a gradient descent step in the parameter space. This isn't actually live yet but the code is just about finished.
